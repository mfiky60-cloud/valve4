Nasser Scoring System (NSS) — External Validation Data Tool

For participating cardiac surgery centres · valve surgery mortality model · data entered here stays on your computer until you export and email the CSV back

📋 Note to the participating centre — please read before you start

Purpose. You are helping to externally validate the Nasser Scoring System (NSS), a mortality risk model for valve surgery developed for rheumatic-predominant populations. We compare the score's prediction against your real outcomes.

  1. Which patients to enter. Consecutive patients aged 16 years or older undergoing valve surgery of any kind — replacement or repair, single or multiple valves, with or without concomitant CABG or other procedures. Exclude valve operations combined with major aortic (thoracic-aorta) surgery, and exclude paediatric or congenital cases (under 16 years, infants, neonates): the NSS is an adult/adolescent model and is not valid for them. Above 80 years, data are sparse — interpret with caution.
  2. One record per operation. Complete every field for each patient, then press “Add patient to dataset”.
  3. Outcome definition. Record the patient as “Died” if death occurred during the same hospital admission OR within 30 days of surgery (whichever applies). Otherwise record “Alive”. Indicate the timing, and if the patient died, the main cause of death. You may enter a patient at any time, even while still alive before day 30. If that patient later dies within 30 days, just re-enter the same study code (or click it in the table) to bring the record back and update it. Only export and send the file once every patient is at least 30 days past surgery — patients still inside the 30-day window are flagged with ⏳ in the table.
  4. Identification & privacy. Use a local study code for each patient (e.g. your centre initials + a sequential number). Do not enter patient names or national ID numbers. Keep your own private log linking the study code to the medical record. Nothing leaves your computer automatically — data is held in this page only.
  5. When finished. Press “Export CSV” to download the file, then email it to the coordinating centre (Nasser Institute, Department of Cardiac Surgery). The CSV contains the variables, the NSS-predicted risk, and your recorded outcome — everything we need to verify the model.
Tip: the predicted mortality updates live as you enter each patient, so the tool doubles as a bedside risk calculator. Save your browser tab; your entered patients persist on this device until you clear them.

Nasser Institute for Research and Treatment · Department of Cardiac Surgery · NSS v2.7.1 external validation (model developed 1998–2023, n=15,206; incorporating preoperative rhythm and admission haemoglobin). v2.7 additionally captures pulmonary artery pressure (PAP, mmHg) and right-ventricular function for prospective collection; these are stored and exported but do not enter the current NSS or EuroSCORE II estimate. Haemoglobin adds no risk at or above 11 g/dL, then steps up by anaemia grade: moderate 8–10.9 g/dL (OR 1.40) and severe <8 g/dL (OR 1.74). Normal/high haemoglobin never lowers risk.
